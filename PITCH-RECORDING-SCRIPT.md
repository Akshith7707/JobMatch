# 4–5 Minute Pitch — What to Say + What to Show  
*Easy English · for screen recording*

**Before you record:** Open the app in the browser. Log in so the dashboard works. Have backend + frontend running. Do a quick practice once without recording.

**Total time:** About 4 to 5 minutes. Speak slowly and clearly. Pause 1 second when you change the page.

---

## Part 1 — Opening (about 45 seconds)

### What to say
- “Hi, I am [your name]. Today I will show you **SkillSync AI** — a tool for **software developers** who want to find **real jobs** that **fit their skills**.”
- “The **problem** is simple: job sites are **noisy**. Same jobs everywhere. Hard to know **what fits you**. ATS systems want **keywords**, not always **real skill**.”
- “SkillSync helps you **see live jobs from the web**, **match them to your profile**, and **prepare** — résumé, cover letter, interview — **in one place**.”

### What to show
- Start on the **home page** (`/`).  
- Scroll a little so they see the hero and one feature block.  
- *Optional:* Point with your cursor at the title “SkillSync” or “Real Jobs.”

---

## Part 2 — The idea in simple words (about 45 seconds)

### What to say
- “**How it works:** You **upload your résumé** and you can add your **GitHub**. Our system **reads your skills** and **experience**.”
- “We also **pull fresh job listings** from places like RemoteOK and other sources — so you are not stuck with old data.”
- “Then **AI matches** you to jobs and shows a **score** and **skill gaps** — so you know **why** a job fits or **what to learn**.”

### What to show
- Stay on **home page**, or move to **Register / Login** for 3 seconds if you want to show sign-up (do not type password on slow typing — you can say “user logs in here”).  
- Best: go to **Login** (`/login`), click in the email field *without* typing the full password on camera — or say “I am already logged in” and cut to dashboard in editing.

---

## Part 3 — Live demo — Dashboard (about 1 minute)

### What to say
- “This is the **dashboard**. Here you see **job matches** ranked for **you** — not a random list.”
- “Each card shows a **match score**, **skills** match, and **actions**: open the real job, **save**, mark **applied**, or **tailor your résumé** for that role.”
- “I can **refresh jobs** to pull the **latest listings** from the web.”

### What to show
- Open **`/dashboard`**.  
- Scroll through **1 or 2 job cards**.  
- Hover over **Tailor**, **Save**, or **Details** — click **Details** or **View Details** to go to job page *or* click **Tailor** if you want to show the modal in Part 4.  
- *If you click Tailor:* say one line — “This opens AI help for that job” — then **continue in Part 4**.

---

## Part 4 — Tailor + extra tools (about 1 minute)

### What to say
- “For any job, I can open **Résumé tailor**. It gives **ATS-style feedback**, **keywords**, and suggestions. I can even **generate a tailored résumé draft** for that job.”
- “We also have **cover letters**, **interview prep**, and a **learning path** for skills I am missing.”
- “There is an **application tracker** — so I can move stages from applied to interview and add **notes**.”

### What to show
- **Tailor modal** on dashboard (if open): show the **scores** at top, **one tab** (Overview or Keywords), then close.  
- Or open **`/jobs/[some-id]`** — show **Cover letter** and **Interview prep** buttons.  
- Quickly open **`/applications`** — scroll the pipeline.  
- Quickly open **`/learning`** — scroll once.  
- Keep each page **5–10 seconds** so you stay under time.

---

## Part 5 — Tech + why it is different (about 45 seconds)

### What to say
- “**Under the hood:** we use **Next.js** for the website, **Node and Express** for the API, and **PostgreSQL** for data — for example **Neon** in the cloud.”
- “AI runs through **Nebius** with models like **Llama** for text and **embeddings** for similarity — so matching is not only keyword search.”
- “What is **different** from a normal job board? We **aggregate live listings**, we **explain the match**, and we **help you act** — tailor, letter, prep — **in one workflow**.”

### What to show
- Optional: **README** or **`pitch.md`** on screen for 5 seconds *only if* your rules allow — or **skip** and stay on **dashboard** or **architecture** slide if you made a slide.  
- If no slide: stay on **dashboard** or **jobs browse** (`/jobs`) while you talk.

---

## Part 6 — Closing (about 30 seconds)

### What to say
- “In short: SkillSync helps developers go from **scattered job posts** to **clear matches** and **strong applications**.”
- “**Thank you** for watching. I am happy to take questions.”

### What to show
- Return to **home page** (`/`) or stay on **dashboard** with a clean view.  
- Smile (if on camera). **Stop recording.**

---

## Quick checklist (print or second screen)

| Time   | Page / screen        | Say (one line)        |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 0:00   | Home                 | Problem + product name |
| 1:00   | Login or Dashboard   | How it works (resume + jobs + AI) |
| 2:00   | Dashboard            | Matches + actions      |
| 3:00   | Tailor / Jobs / Apps | Tailor + tracker + learning |
| 4:00   | Any + voice          | Stack + USP              |
| 4:30   | Home or Dashboard    | Thank you                |

---

## Tips for recording

- **Speak** like you talk to a friend — not too fast.  
- **Do not** read long paragraphs; use the bullets above as **memory hooks**.  
- If you **make a mistake**, pause, say “Again,” and repeat that **one sentence** — cut in edit.  
- **Sound:** quiet room, mic close to mouth.  
- **Screen:** 1920×1080 or 1280×720, browser **full screen**, **zoom 100%**.

Good luck with your submission.
